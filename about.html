The Soul

Our Beginning

Born from a
Thousand Journeys

Void was born in 2021 from a single obsession: what if Kathmandu could taste the full breadth of Asia — not a watered-down, tourist-friendly approximation, but the real, raw, soulful food of Japan's izakayas, Thailand's night markets, and China's ancestral kitchens?

Our founder spent four years eating her way through Asia — slurping ramen at 3am in Osaka, sweating over a clay pot in Chiang Mai, arguing with a Cantonese uncle about the correct roasting time for duck. Every meal became a lesson. Every lesson became Void.

Nestled in the spiritual heart of Boudha, we believe the act of eating together is sacred — as sacred as the chanting just outside our doors. We cook with that reverence in every pot.

What We Stand For

The Principles of Void

01

Authenticity Above All

We refuse shortcuts. Traditional methods, ancestral spice blends, and imported ingredients ensure every dish is the real thing — not a replica.

02

Seasonal & Fresh

Our menu shifts with what the season offers. Kalimati market at dawn, farm deliveries, and zero frozen stock — freshness is non-negotiable.

03

Generous Hospitality

In Asian culture, feeding someone is an act of love. We carry that spirit into every interaction — from the first bow to the last chopstick rest.

04

Community Roots

Boudha shaped us. We source locally, hire locally, and give back to the neighbourhood that embraced us from day one.

Meet Our Chefs

Executive Chef

Chef Aiko Tanaka

Trained at Culinary Institute of Japan in Osaka and later under legendary Chef Nobu Matsuhisa in Bangkok, Aiko brings a philosophy of mu — nothingness, precision, perfect negative space — to every dish she creates at Void. Her duck is spoken of in whispers across Kathmandu.

Japanese Kaiseki Fermentation Umami Craft

Sous Chef

Chef Somchai Phuket

Born into a family of Thai street vendors in Chiang Mai, Somchai learned to build flavour from nothing — a handful of herbs, a charcoal grill, and time. He brings that energy, heat, and explosive spice to the Thai and Chinese sections of our menu.

Thai Chinese Open Flame Spice Curation
Our Journey

The Milestones
That Made Us

Every restaurant has a story. Ours is written in spice stains, late-night recipe arguments, and the faces of guests who tasted something that reminded them of home.

2018

The Idea is Born

Chef Aiko eats her way through 6 Asian countries. A napkin scrawl in a Kyoto izakaya becomes the blueprint for Void.

2020

A Home in Boudha

After months searching Kathmandu, a double-storey space in Boudha-4 is chosen — close to the stupa, close to the soul of the city.

2021

Opening Night

Void opens its doors. Within three weeks, every weekend table is booked a month in advance. The city is hungry for this.

2023

Nepal Food Awards

Awarded "Best Asian Restaurant in Kathmandu" by Nepal Culinary Society. The Black Truffle Duck named Dish of the Year.

2025

Family Expansion

New family-dining section opens with dedicated children's menu, weekend dim sum brunch, and private dining events.
